Property Law in Zambia

Property law in Zambia protects your right to own, use, and transfer property. It covers land, homes, livestock, personal items, and even things like phones or furniture.

Types of Property

  • Movable property: These are items you can carry or move — phones, clothes, furniture, money, etc.
  • Immovable property: Land and anything permanently attached to land, like houses or trees.

Ways You Can Own Property

You can legally own property in the following ways:

  • Individual ownership: Owned by one person.
  • Joint ownership: Owned by more than one person — common in marriages or family land.
  • Leasehold ownership: You get land or property for a period (usually up to 99 years), especially on state land.
  • Customary use: Land allocated by traditional leaders under customary law.

Your Rights as a Property Owner

  • Use and enjoy the property without interference.
  • Sell, give away, rent, or leave your property in a will.
  • Protect it from theft or damage by going to court or police.

When Property Is Shared

In marriages and families, property may be jointly owned — both partners may have a claim. If a couple divorces or one dies, courts may divide property based on who contributed or what’s fair.

Common Property Disputes

  • Inheritance: Who gets what after someone dies?
  • Eviction: Can a landlord remove a tenant without proper notice? (Answer: No)
  • Family land conflicts: Often arise in rural areas over customary land use or boundaries.
  • Property taken as collateral: Lenders must follow the law before repossessing.

If Someone Steals or Destroys Your Property

You can report the matter to the police or open a civil case in local or magistrate courts. Make sure to keep records of ownership like receipts, agreements, or witnesses.
