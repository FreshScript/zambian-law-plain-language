Police and Your Rights in Zambia

In Zambia, the police are meant to protect life, property, and the law. But every citizen has rights that must be respected, even when the police are doing their job.

When You’re Stopped by Police

You may be stopped while walking, driving, or at a roadblock. In these situations:

  • Stay calm and respectful.
  • You can ask: “Why am I being stopped?”
  • You’re only required to provide your name, national registration card (NRC), or driving license if asked.
  • You have the right to be spoken to politely and not be harassed or insulted.

Search and Seizure

  • Police need a **search warrant** to enter your home, unless there’s an emergency (e.g., someone’s in danger).
  • They can only search your body, car, or bag if they have reasonable cause — and they should explain why.
  • Women should be searched by female officers whenever possible.

When You Are Arrested

If you're arrested, here’s what must happen:

  • The officer must tell you why you’re being arrested.
  • You have the right to remain silent — you don’t have to answer questions without a lawyer.
  • You must be taken to court within 48 hours. Longer than that is unlawful unless a judge approves it.

Your Rights in Police Custody

  • You have a right to call a relative or lawyer.
  • You must be treated with dignity — no beatings, torture, or inhumane conditions.
  • Children or minors must be separated from adult suspects.

Right to Bail

For most charges, you can apply for bail while your case is ongoing. Serious offences like murder may not qualify for bail. Bail may be granted by a police officer, magistrate, or judge.

If Your Rights Are Violated

If you’re abused by a police officer:

  • Report to the Police Public Complaints Commission (PPCC).
  • You can also take civil legal action for compensation through the courts.
  • Human rights groups and legal aid offices can support your case if you cannot afford a lawyer.

Your Role as a Citizen

While the police must act lawfully, citizens also have responsibilities:

  • Follow lawful instructions during a search or arrest.
  • Do not obstruct officers in the line of duty.
  • Report crime or misconduct responsibly and truthfully.
